# Relevance Criteria

## Update Urgency Levels

### High (act within 1 week)
- Our content contains factually incorrect information
- A practice we recommend has been shown to cause harm
- A critical tool/API has changed in ways that break our guidance

### Medium (act within 1 month)
- A better practice has emerged with strong evidence
- A new tool capability changes the recommended workflow
- Multiple sources converge on a point we don't cover

### Low (quarterly review)
- Wording improvements
- Additional examples or case studies
- Minor clarifications

## Evidence Standards

### Strong Evidence (high confidence)
- Peer-reviewed research or controlled experiments
- Official documentation from tool makers
- Consistent reports from 3+ independent practitioners

### Moderate Evidence (medium confidence)
- Single well-documented case study
- Expert opinion from recognized practitioners
- Community consensus (multiple Reddit/Twitter threads)

### Weak Evidence (low confidence)
- Single anecdotal report
- Promotional content
- Unverified claims

## Decision Rules

- Only recommend "update" or "remove" with strong or moderate evidence
- "Add" can be based on moderate evidence if the topic is clearly missing
- "Reinforce" can be based on any evidence level
- When evidence conflicts, note the conflict rather than picking a side
